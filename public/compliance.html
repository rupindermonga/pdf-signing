PIPEDA — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act

SealForge handles personal information (names, email addresses, signatures, IP addresses, geolocation) as a service provider on behalf of our customers. We follow the ten fair-information principles set out in PIPEDA Schedule 1:

  • Accountability — we maintain a privacy contact (see below) and are responsible for personal information under our control.
  • Identifying purposes — personal information is collected only to deliver e-signature, audit-trail, and verification services.
  • Consent — signers consent to processing each time they verify their email and submit a signature.
  • Limiting collection — we collect the minimum needed: signer name, email, IP, optional location, browser fingerprint, and the signature image.
  • Limiting use, disclosure, retention — data is used only to fulfill the signing request and is not sold or shared with third parties for marketing.
  • Accuracy — signers verify their own information at the time of signing.
  • Safeguards — see Security below.
  • Openness — this page documents our practices.
  • Individual access — signers may request a copy of their data via the privacy contact.
  • Challenging compliance — concerns may be addressed to our privacy contact and, if unresolved, to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

Electronic-signature laws

SealForge-generated signatures are designed to satisfy the legal requirements of:

  • PIPEDA Part 2 (federal e-document framework) — secure electronic signatures with signer identity, intent, and association to the document.
  • UECA / provincial e-signature acts (BC, Alberta, Ontario, Saskatchewan, etc.) and Quebec's Act to establish a legal framework for IT.
  • U.S. ESIGN Act & UETA for cross-border use.
  • EU eIDAS "advanced electronic signature" — when a qualified PKCS#12 certificate is configured.
Note on QES. The standard SealForge certificate produces an advanced electronic signature. To produce a qualified electronic signature (QES) under eIDAS, a Qualified Trust Service Provider certificate must be configured.

Data residency

By default, SealForge stores all uploaded PDFs, signature images, and audit data on the server you have deployed it to. When self-hosted in a Canadian region, all customer data remains on Canadian soil at rest and in transit, satisfying provincial data-residency requirements such as BC FIPPA s. 30.1 and Quebec Law 25.

SaaS users: the production region is documented in your service agreement. Contact us to confirm your active region.

Security controls

  • HTTPS with HSTS, X-Frame-Options DENY, strict Content-Security-Policy.
  • Session cookies: HttpOnly, SameSite=Lax, secure in production.
  • Password-less email-OTP authentication (10-minute expiry, timing-safe comparison).
  • Rate limiting on OTP and signing endpoints.
  • SHA-256 hashing of every original PDF on upload.
  • PKCS#7 / X.509 cryptographic digital signature on completed PDFs.
  • API keys hashed (SHA-256) at rest; plaintext shown only at creation time.
  • Webhooks signed with HMAC-SHA256 (X-SealForge-Signature header).
  • SSRF protections on outbound webhook calls (private IP ranges blocked).

Audit trail

Every completed document includes a Certificate of Signing page recording, for each signer:

  • Document UUID and SHA-256 hash of the original
  • Signer name, email, role, signing order
  • Signed-at timestamp (UTC)
  • IP address (public IP when reported by client)
  • Approximate geolocation (when permission granted)
  • Browser and operating system

The audit page is part of the signed PDF and protected by the same digital signature as the rest of the document — any tampering breaks the signature.

Retention & deletion

Documents and audit trails are retained for the lifetime of the customer account. Customers may delete documents from their dashboard at any time; deletion is immediate and irreversible. On account closure, all customer data is deleted within 30 days unless a longer retention period is required by applicable law.
